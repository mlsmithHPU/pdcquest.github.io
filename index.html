PDC Quest Card Game

PDC Quest is a card game that includes 80 cards (questions) based on the eight PDC (Parallel and Distributed Computing) concept categories. Each card is numbered and coded according to its category, with each category having a unique number (1–8) and a two-letter abbreviation.


PDC Concept Categories

PDC Concept Category Code
1. Concurrency CC
2. Mutual exclusion ME
3. Consistency in state/memory manipulation CM
4. Message-passing MP
5. Shared-memory models SM
6. Scalability SC
7. Scheduling and load balancing SL
8. Speedup / Amdahl's law SA

Each category includes ten cards, resulting in a total of 80 cards. PDC Quest can be played after covering one or more PDC concept categories in class, making it a great review activity before quizzes or exams. It also serves as an engaging way to evaluate students’ grasp of PDC concepts. The game is designed for groups of three players, but it can be easily modified to accommodate larger groups.

PDC Quest Game Rules

Set Up

  • Before starting, select the category (or categories) of cards to be reviewed.
  • Place these cards into the Deal stack and shuffle thoroughly.

Taking Turns

  • Player 1 draws the top card from the Deal stack and passes it to Player 2.
  • Player 2 reads the question aloud while Player 1 has 60 seconds to answer.

Scoring

  • After Player 1 responds, Players 2 and 3 evaluate the answer and assign a score:
    • 0 = no answer or incorrect
    • 1 = partially correct
    • 2 = fully correct
  • Players may consult the QR code for more details, discuss the topic, and use the conversation as part of the learning process.
  • Record Player 1’s score on the scoresheet, then place the card in the Played stack.

Continuing Play

  • Player 2 draws a card and passes it to Player 3, who reads the question aloud.
  • Player 2 answers; Players 1 and 3 evaluate and score the response.
  • Continue in rotation until the Deal stack is empty.

Recording Results

  • The scoresheet should include each player's name, the categories reviewed, and the scores earned.
  • At the end of the game, calculate each player's average score per category:
Average = Total Score in Category ÷ Number of Cards Played in Category

Use the score sheet to identify which categories require further review.
