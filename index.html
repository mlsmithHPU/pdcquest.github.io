PDC Quest Card Game

PDC Quest is a card game that includes 80 cards (questions) based on the eight PDC (Parallel and Distributed Computing) concept categories. Each card is numbered and coded according to its category, with each category having a unique number (1–8) and a two-letter abbreviation.


PDC Concept Categories

PDC Concept Category Code
1. Concurrency CC
2. Mutual exclusion ME
3. Consistency in state/memory manipulation CM
4. Message-passing MP
5. Shared-memory models SM
6. Scalability SC
7. Scheduling and load balancing SL
8. Speedup / Amdahl's law SA

Each category includes ten cards, resulting in a total of 80 cards. PDC Quest can be played after covering one or more PDC concept categories in class, making it a great review activity before quizzes or exams. It also serves as an engaging way to evaluate students’ grasp of PDC concepts. The game is designed for groups of three players, but it can be easily modified to accommodate larger groups.

PDC Quest Game Rules

Before the game begins, the category or categories of cards to be reviewed should be pulled from the main deck and placed in the Deal stack. Ensure that the Deal stack is thoroughly shuffled. To begin the game, Player 1 selects a card from the Deal stack and passes it to Player 2. Player 2 reads the question aloud, waiting for Player 1 to answer. Player 1 has 60 seconds to provide an answer. Upon Player 1 answering the question, Players 2 and 3 evaluate and rate the answer given by Player 1 by assigning a score of 0 for no answer or incorrect answer, 1 for a partially correct answer, and a score of 2 for a complete and correct answer. The evaluation process could involve examining a more comprehensive description of the subject matter via the QR code, prompting a discussion among the players and contributing to their learning experience. The score for Player 1 should be recorded on the scoresheet, and the card is placed in another stack called the Played stack.

The game continues as Player 2 draws from the Deal stack and gives the card to Player 3 to read aloud, and Player 2 answers the question. Players 1 and 3 evaluate the answer, assign a score by documenting it on the scoresheet, and place the card played in the Played stack. The game continues in this manner until no more cards are left in the Deal stack. The scoresheet is kept, which indicates the players' names, categories, and scores received. An example of the scoresheet is shown in Figure 1. At the end of the game, players average their scores from each category by taking the total score from each category and dividing it by the number of cards played in that category. The scoresheet can act as a gauge of revealing which categories the player needs to study more.
